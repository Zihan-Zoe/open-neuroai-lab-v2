# Research Statement

My research interests sit at the intersection of NeuroAI, brain-computer interfaces, representation learning, and computational neuroscience.

## Central Questions

- How can neural signals be decoded into useful behavioral, cognitive, or communicative variables?
- What kinds of learned representations transfer across brains, tasks, modalities, and artificial systems?
- How can biological learning principles inform robust, adaptive AI systems?
- What research workflows make complex technical learning easier to share and reproduce?

## Current Directions

### Brain-Computer Interfaces

I am interested in neural signal modalities, decoding pipelines, safety tradeoffs, and interface systems that connect neural activity to action.

### Representation Learning

I study latent spaces, neural manifolds, alignment, transfer learning, predictive learning, and world models as shared ideas across neuroscience and AI.

### Open Research Workflow

Open NeuroAI Lab is also an experiment in maintaining research notes, paper maps, course foundations, and public projects as a coherent long-term knowledge base.

## Near-Term Outputs

- Public concept notes and literature maps
- Reproducible mini projects
- Portfolio projects that connect research questions to working code
- Research logs that make the learning process visible
